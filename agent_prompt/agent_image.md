Create one complete paid social advertising image that allows the viewer to understand within one second:

1. what is being advertised
2. the main benefit, problem, proof, or offer

==================================================
TYPOGRAPHY ART DIRECTION
==================================================

Typography must behave as part of the composition.

Treat the headline as a visual shape.

Specify:

- font personality
- headline size
- weight
- line-breaking logic
- alignment
- width of the text block
- emphasized keywords
- relation to the hero
- support-copy placement
- offer placement
- CTA placement

Use no more than two primary type personalities.

Emphasize no more than two words or phrases using:

- size
- weight
- brand color
- placement

Do not emphasize every word.

Use line breaks based on meaning and rhythm, not simply available space.

The headline block should help balance or direct attention toward the hero.

Create this reading sequence:

1. headline or main offer
2. hero visual
3. benefit, proof, or supporting message
4. CTA
5. secondary information
6. logo

At mobile thumbnail size, the viewer must still identify:

- the product or service
- the main headline or offer
- the CTA or next action

Avoid:

- excessive outlines
- random drop shadows
- fake chrome text
- uncontrolled bevels
- arbitrary curved text
- multiple font styles
- every word using a different color
- unreadable experimental letterforms

==================================================
THAI TEXT ACCURACY
==================================================

When Thai text is supplied:

- use only the exact provided copy
- do not rewrite or paraphrase
- do not invent filler text
- preserve all spelling
- preserve punctuation
- preserve numbers and symbols
- preserve consonants, vowels, and tone marks
- do not replace Thai characters
- use line breaks based on meaning
- do not stretch or distort Thai letters
- keep critical copy large enough for mobile reading
- render each mandatory message only once unless repetition is explicitly required

Treat all Thai text as final approved artwork copy.

Use simpler typography effects when decorative treatment may reduce Thai accuracy.

==================================================
BACKGROUND FUNCTION
==================================================

The background must perform one primary function only:

- establish usage context
- build a branded world
- reinforce the visual metaphor
- create contrast for typography
- provide technical context
- add controlled spatial depth

Do not use a decorative gradient, landscape, room, or abstract scene with no relationship to the selling message.

Control background detail:

- keep typography areas cleaner
- allow more information near the hero only when necessary
- reduce contrast in secondary areas
- keep the background less dominant than the hero

Negative space must feel intentionally shaped, not unfinished.

Reject and redesign the concept internally if it contains any of the following without a strategic reason:

- product floating over a generic gradient
- product on one side and text on the other with no visual relationship
- image on top and solid text block below
- hard 50/50 split layout
- centered product surrounded by icons
- excessive rounded cards
- random neon glow
- glossy plastic 3D objects
- floating geometric shapes
- meaningless particles
- fake futuristic UI
- excessive lens flare
- excessive bokeh
- unreadable packaging labels
- duplicated products
- incorrect anatomy
- inconsistent shadows
- unrelated scenery
- stock-photo expressions
- visual clutter without hierarchy
- every element centered
- every word styled differently
- product too small to recognize
- CTA hidden near an edge
- logo larger than the selling message
- typography that appears pasted over the image
- image, people, products, and background that feel generated separately
- a composition that could be reused unchanged for another brand

The final design must be specific to the brand, product, audience, and selling message.
